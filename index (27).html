SpeechyAI
Mission Statement

SpeechyAI was designed to make speech therapy diagnostics accessible and personal to assist speech language pathologists when observing one's lateral /s/ speech impediment.

How To Use

SpeechyAI is a tool designed for individuals with lateral /s/ lisps. It observes a sound's clarity using advanced mathematical techniques and gives a score for conversational clarity, then gives a final assessment on the severity of the lisp based on the /s/ sound. To use SpeechyAI, follow the steps below.

  1. First, launch SpeechyAI below under the "Try It Out" section and click "Let's Go" after Speechy prompts you.
  2. Move to a quiet space and either record or upload yourself, your child, or your patient saying a word with an /s/ sound. Some examples include snake, soup, sell, sock, and sign. When saying the word, the user should say it normally and not hold the /s/ sound extra long because Speechy will be more critical of the sound. Speechy specializes in conversational speaking, not sustained sounds. Speechy can also analyze /s/ blend sounds. Speechy will not give an accurate assessment if the user does not include an /s/ sound. If the user says a word like rope, for example, then Speechy would not work properly.
  3. After the user records or uploads their sound, they are prompted to trim out all remaining noise that is not the /s/. For example, if the user says soup, they would trim out the /oup/ and any noise that occurred before the /s/ sound. This allows Speechy to isolate the /s/ and provide the best analysis it can.
  4. Speechy will analyze the speech and generate a quantitative clarity score for the lateral /s/, reflecting how the sound presents in conversation. It will then provide a qualitative severity score determining the clarity level of the lisp, along with exercise suggestions to help the user potentially improve their clarity. The most important aspect of the scores is that they are personal. Speechy is not designed to compare scores from person to person; comparing an adult male's speech clarity to a young female's is like comparing apples to oranges. Speechy's score is a personal reference point. If the user consistently works with a speech language pathologist and reassesses over time, improving scores indicate a clearer /s/ sound.
